## Project Summary

Project name: DexHand Lab

Robothon platform: MuJoCo dexterous hand simulation

Final submission folder: `submissions/dexhand_lab/`

Task goal: demonstrate a hand-only human-like 5-finger robot that performs object-specific grasping, in-hand manipulation, tactile evidence logging, blind tactile active perception, no-ground-truth tactile pose estimation, precision plug/socket assembly, adaptive regrasp, cap rotation, slip recovery, stylus interaction, and index-only button pressing.

DexHand Lab is a custom MuJoCo benchmark centered on a skeletal 5-finger hand rather than a robot arm. The hand has visible thumb opposition, long-finger MCP/PIP/DIP-style joints, fingertip pads, tactile/contact evidence streams, and object-specific grasp primitives for sphere, cube, cylinder, cap/knob, stylus, and button tasks.

## What This Submission Demonstrates

- Human-like 5-finger skeletal hand with visible thumb opposition.
- Independent finger timing and role-specific motion.
- Sphere enclosure grasp with multi-side contact.
- Cube opposing-face grasp instead of corner grasp.
- Cylinder side-body grasp instead of top-down grasp.
- In-hand cylinder rotation with finger-gaiting metrics.
- Signature 224-degree cap/knob rotation with visible marker.
- Blind tactile active perception where object labels are hidden and the hand probes before selecting a grasp.
- No-ground-truth tactile pose estimation where exact object pose is hidden from the controller and used only after the episode for scoring.
- Precision assembly arena where the hand estimates a plug/key pose, aligns it with a socket, detects jamming, withdraws/corrects, retries insertion, and verifies final insertion depth.
- Adaptive regrasp after low confidence, contact imbalance, slip, or suspected mismatch.
- Slip recovery and 9x load-hold evidence.
- Stylus tripod grasp and checkpoint touch.
- Index-only button press.
- Contact timeline, tactile taxel streams, minimum-jerk report, stress evaluation, baseline comparison, and hardware replay audit.

## Technical Approach

- Custom primitive MJCF hand model, avoiding fragile external mesh dependencies.
- Five fingertip tactile channels using MuJoCo touch/contact parsing and controller pressure proxies.
- Simulation-native object pose validation, not camera-based perception.
- Human grasp library with sphere enclosure, cube opposing-face, cylinder side-body, tripod precision, button press, cap twist, slip recovery, and controlled release primitives.
- No-snap verified grasp routine: objects are only carried or rotated after multi-finger contact and stability verification.
- Tactile-inspired minimum-jerk segments for smooth approach, contact seek, twist, recovery, load hold, and release.
- Deterministic tactile pose estimator and compliant assembly controller for plug/socket insertion with jam recovery.
- 20-gate deterministic task suite and 32-seed stress evaluation.
- Hardware-transfer-style replay audit, documented as simulation replay rather than a physical hardware trial.

## Honest Limitations

- The controller is deterministic and contact-aware, not learned RL.
- Perception is simulation-native and tactile/contact-derived, not real camera vision.
- In no-ground-truth pose mode, exact pose is hidden from controller decisions and used only for scoring/audit after the episode.
- Tactile values are MuJoCo touch/contact/controller proxies, not physical hardware tactile readings.
- Hybrid carry and cap rotation are used after contact verification for reproducibility.
- Hardware adaptation is a replay/safety audit, not a physical hardware trial.
